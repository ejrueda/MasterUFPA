El análisis de la expresión génica es uno de los estudios más comúnes e importantes en biología y biomedicina.
No importa el análisis de transferencia tradicional o la estrategia de PCR utilizada comúnmente en la actualidad, 
todos necesitan un gen de referencia estable para normalizar la expresión génica. Para seleccionar y seleccionar
genes de mantenimiento como los genes de referencia más estables, se empleó PCR cuantitativa en tiempo real (qRT-PCR)
para analizar la expresión de dieciséis genes de referencia comúnmente utilizados (IbelF, Ibα-tubulin, IbHIS, IbCOX,
IbGAPDH, IbH2B1, IbARF, IbCYC, Ibβ-tubulina, IbACT, IbEFl-a, IbG14, IbPLD, IbRPL2, IbUBQ, IbUBI) en cinco tejidos
diferentes bajo dos tensiones de temperatura diferentes en la batata. El análisis de datos realizado por los programas
Delta CT, geNorm, NormFinder y BestKeeper reveló que IbelF es el gen más estable e IbUBI es el gen menos estable como
referencia. Nuestro estudio también muestra que la combinación de dos o más genes como referencia es una mejor opción,
lo que hace que los datos de expresión más justificados para la comparación. Este estudio proporciona evidencia para
seleccionar genes de referencia en el análisis de expresión génica de batata.

Although real-time RT-PCR is widely used to quantitate biologically relevant changes in mRNA levels, there remain a number of problems
associated with its use. These include the inherent variability of RNA, variability of extraction protocols that may copurify inhibitors,
and different reverse transcription and PCR efficiencies.4 Consequently, it is important that an accurate method of normalisation is chosen
to control for this error. Unfortunately, normalisation remains one of real-time RT-PCRs most difficult problems.5

Several strategies have been proposed for normalising real-time RT-PCR data. These range from ensuring that a similar sample size is chosen
to using an internal housekeeping or reference gene (Table 1). These approaches are not mutually exclusive and can be incorporated into a
protocol at many stages (Figure 1). Here we discuss the respective advantages and disadvantages of each technique.

Normalising to a reference gene is a simple and popular method for internally controlling for error in real-time RT-PCR. This strategy targets
RNAs encoded by genes, which have been collectively called housekeeping genes and benefits from the fact that all the steps required to obtain
the final PCR measurement are controlled for. The procedure is simplified as both the gene of interest and the reference gene are measured using
real-time RT-PCR. Reference genes can also control for different input RNA amounts used in the reverse transcription step (Figure 5); however,
because this can vary with reverse transcriptase type7 it must be validated.

Genes de referência:
The most important is its expression level unaffected by experimental factors. Also, it should show minimal variability in its expression
between tissues and physiological states of the organism. It is desirable to pick such reference that would show a similar threshold cycle
with gene of interest. Reference gene must in turn demonstrate the variability resulting from imperfections of the technology used and
preparatory procedures—this ensures that any variation in the amount of genetic material will relate to the same extent as the object of
research and control.  (https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s13353-013-0173-x)
